# Owner asset checklist

Send this list to the brand owner. Until these arrive, the site renders with placeholders (CSS gradients, SVG mock pack, stock-style filler copy).

## What to send

1. **Five websites you love** (any genre — fashion, design, food, tech)
2. **Five websites you hate**
3. **Three scroll animations you like** (link, screen recording, or short video clip)
4. **Product photos**
   - Front, back, side of every SKU
   - Product on white background
   - Product in hand
   - Product with whisk and bowl
   - Macro shot of matcha powder
5. **Packaging files** (logo SVG/PNG, label artwork)
6. **Brand colors** (hex codes if known) and **logo** (SVG preferred)
7. **Matcha origin / story** — where leaves are sourced, harvest details, anything specific you want named on the site (region, farm, grade, certifications)
8. **Product prices** for each SKU
9. **Who buys this matcha** — one or two short paragraphs describing the typical customer
10. **Words / vibes you never want on the site** — e.g. "wellness", "zen", "superfood", emoji-heavy copy, anything that's been used to death

## Bonus

- **Matcha videos**: pouring a latte, whisking with chasen — even 5 seconds at 30fps is enough.
- **Tea field / origin photography** if you have any rights-cleared shots.
- **Lab tests / JAS certification PDFs** for the trust strip.

## Format

- Photos: prefer 3000px+ on the long edge, JPG or PNG.
- Video: prefer ProRes or MP4 (H.264/H.265) at the original frame rate.
- Logo and labels: SVG > PNG > JPG.
- Drop everything in a shared folder (Drive / Dropbox / WeTransfer link is fine).

## Why each item matters

| # | Why |
|---|---|
| 1–3 | Sets the visual and motion direction without subjective back-and-forth. |
| 4–5 | Without product photography the hero is built around a placeholder canister. |
| 6 | Final palette can shift slightly if logo lockup demands it. |
| 7 | The scroll story (origin → ritual) needs a real story. |
| 8 | Product cards can't ship without prices. |
| 9 | Tone of voice is calibrated to the customer, not the founder. |
| 10 | Faster to know what to avoid than to keep iterating away from it. |
